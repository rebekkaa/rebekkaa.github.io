About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in Software Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, where I am part of the ABLE Research Group. Before starting at CMU, I was affiliated with Chalmers University of Technology in Gothenburg, Sweden, where I also received my PhD. My research is supported by a WASP Postdoctoral Scholarship by the Knut and Alice Wallenberg Foundation.

I am interested in software architecture, continuous software engineering, requirements engineering, and self-adaptive systems. I have developed the concept of Living Boundary Objects, which focuses on documents or models that support coordination between multidisciplinary teams and create a common understanding in large-scale systems development. Many of my studies have been performed in collaboration with automotive companies.

You can reach me at wohlrab@cmu.edu
